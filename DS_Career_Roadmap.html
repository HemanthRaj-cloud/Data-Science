Interview Preparation Strategy

FAANG DS interviews have 4 core pillars — each requires a separate strategy

💻 Coding / Algorithms
Python-focused DSA. FAANG DS roles test easier than SWE roles but still require solid fundamentals.
Arrays & StringsHash Maps Two PointersSliding Window RecursionBFS / DFS Dynamic Programming
Target: LeetCode Easy (all) + Medium (70%) in 8 weeks
📐 Statistics & ML Theory
The deepest and most unique part of DS interviews — you must explain concepts clearly and from first principles.
Why Gradient Descent?Bias vs Variance Regularization L1/L2AUC-ROC explained Class ImbalanceOverfitting solutions
Practice explaining every concept to a non-technical person
📊 SQL Technical Round
Usually a separate 45-min round. Expect medium-to-hard analytical SQL problems similar to real product data.
User RetentionFunnel Analysis Rolling AveragesCohort Analysis Ranking with RANK()LEAD/LAG
Do 5+ StrataScratch problems per week for 8 weeks
💼 Product & Case Study
Given a business problem — define metrics, design experiments, interpret results. Tests data intuition + business thinking.
Define Success MetricsRoot Cause Analysis Metric Trade-offsExperiment Design Guarding Metrics
Use the STAR + Metric Framework for every case question
💻 LeetCode Study Plan — 8-Week Sprint
  • 1
    Week 1–2: All Easy problems in Arrays, Strings, and Hash MapsEasy — solve 5 problems/day. Focus on clarity, not speed.
  • 2
    Week 3–4: Two Pointers, Sliding Window, and SortingMedium — these appear most in DS coding screens.
  • 3
    Week 5–6: Binary Search, Recursion, Tree traversal (BFS/DFS)Medium — 3 problems/day, review solutions after 20 min max.
  • 4
    Week 7–8: Dynamic Programming basics + mock interviews on Pramp/Interviewing.ioHard — do 2 full mock sessions per week.
  • 5
    Ongoing: Complete LeetCode Top 50 SQL and StrataScratch FAANG SQL set. Target: 100+ SQL problems before first application.
📐 Top Statistics & ML Concepts to Master
Statistics Must-Knows
  • AExplain p-value, Type I/II errors to a PM
  • BCentral Limit Theorem — why it matters in A/B tests
  • CWhen to use t-test vs. Mann-Whitney vs. chi-square
  • DConfidence intervals — construction and interpretation
  • EBayesian vs. Frequentist A/B testing trade-offs
ML Theory Must-Knows
  • ABias-variance tradeoff with examples
  • BWhy regularization works (L1 vs L2 intuition)
  • CHow gradient boosting works vs random forest
  • DHandling class imbalance (SMOTE, weights, thresholds)
  • EFeature importance methods (Shapley, permutation)
💼 Product Case Study — METRICS Framework
Use this structured approach for every product case question. Practice it until it becomes instinct.
THE FRAMEWORK
  • Mission — clarify the business goal
  • Explore — understand the product/data
  • Target metrics — define primary & guardrails
  • Reasoning — explain your approach
  • Interpret — analyze results, edge cases
  • Communicate — summarize clearly
  • Scale — how would this work at 10x?
EXAMPLE QUESTIONS TO PRACTICE
  • →"Instagram engagement is down 10% — diagnose"
  • →"How would you measure success for a new Uber feature?"
  • →"Design an A/B test for a new checkout flow"
  • →"Amazon Prime retention dropped — what do you do?"
  • →"How do you measure the ROI of a recommendation model?"
  • →"Define metrics for Google Maps ETA accuracy"

Networking & Application Tactics

80% of FAANG offers come through referrals — this section is as important as technical prep

🔴 Key Insight
A referral from a current employee can jump you past 80% of applicants in FAANG hiring. Spend at least 30% of your prep time on networking.
🔗
1. LinkedIn Optimization (Week 1 — Do This First)
Update your headline to: "Data Scientist | ML/NLP/Big Data | Python · SQL · Spark | FAU M.S."
Write an "About" section with your value proposition in 3 sentences. Add every project, certification, and tool keyword. Turn on "Open to Work" (recruiter-only). Connect with 10+ DS professionals at target companies each week. Post weekly about your projects, learnings, or data insights — consistency beats virality.
🎓
2. Leverage FAU Alumni Network Strategically
Search LinkedIn for FAU alumni working at Google, Meta, Amazon, Netflix, LinkedIn, Uber. Send personalized connection requests: "Hi [Name], fellow FAU alum here — I noticed your DS work at [company]. I'm targeting DS roles there. Would love 15 min to hear about your experience." Aim for 3-5 alumni coffees per week. Always end with: "Is there anyone else I should speak to?" and "Would you be comfortable referring me?"
🏆
3. Build Presence on Kaggle & GitHub
Kaggle: Enter 2-3 competitions and aim for top 15% — even bronze medals are resume-worthy. GitHub: Make your 5 portfolio projects public with detailed READMEs, demo GIFs, and architecture diagrams. Pin your best 6 repos. Maintain a daily commit streak — recruiters check your GitHub activity graph. Write Medium articles about your projects — these often rank on Google and lead to inbound recruiter messages.
📬
4. Smart Application Strategy
Don't spray and pray. Apply to 5-10 roles per week, all highly targeted. Customize your resume for each job description using the exact keywords from the JD. Use Teal HQ or Huntr to track applications. Apply on company career pages AND LinkedIn. Timing matters: Apply on Tuesday or Wednesday mornings for highest recruiter visibility. Follow up with a recruiter on LinkedIn 5-7 days after applying: "Hi [Name], I applied for DS role #XXXXX. My background in Big Data and ML aligns directly with the JD — happy to share more."
🎤
5. Attend Meetups & Conferences
In-person: Find local Data Science meetups (Meetup.com), Kaggle Days, PyData conferences. Virtual: NeurIPS, ICML, MLConf, Data + AI Summit (Databricks), AWS re:Invent. Volunteer at events — you get free entry + direct access to speakers who are often FAANG employees. Ask speakers thoughtful questions after talks — this is how cold connections turn warm.
💰
6. Use Referral Platforms
Blind: Use the anonymous "Referrals" section to ask for referrals at specific companies. ReferralHero / Teamblind Referrals: Many employees post referral offers — respond promptly with your resume. Reddit: r/cscareerquestions and r/datascience frequently have referral threads. Discord: MLOps Community, Hugging Face, and various DS Discords have #job-referrals channels.
